Issues Being Addressed
Fragmented CI/CD ownership with multiple team-managed runners and inconsistent pipeline implementations
High operational toil due to infrastructure management, runner issues, and manual secrets handling
Inconsistent security and compliance enforcement, including manual SAST evidence submission
Lack of standardisation in pipelines leading to duplication and increased cognitive load
Slow onboarding and setup for new services and environments
Limited visibility into pipeline performance, failures, and cost usage
Uncontrolled change and release processes with manual approvals and inconsistent gate enforcement
Inefficient resource utilisation due to idle or over-provisioned runners

Comment / Status
Migration to central GitHub Actions runner platform in progress, establishing a standardised CI/CD execution model
CI/CD infrastructure abstracted into a managed platform, eliminating non-value-adding operational work for application teams
Security and compliance controls embedded into pipelines, shifting enforcement left and eliminating manual intervention
Reusable workflows and standard pipeline patterns introduced to improve consistency and reduce cognitive complexity
Automated onboarding and pre-approved templates enabling faster and consistent service setup
Centralised observability introduced for CI/CD pipelines, enabling proactive monitoring and reliability improvements
Automated change gates and controlled release workflows implemented, aligning deployments with policy-driven governance
Autoscaling runner model implemented to optimise utilisation and align capacity with demand
